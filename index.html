About the Project

Welcome to my summer project focused on Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV) and Particle Tracking Velocimetry (PTV) in multiphase flow in porous media.

The UiO:Energy and Environment Initiative funds research projects within the field of energy, climate and environment. This project was proposed and supervised by researchers at PoreLab and the Njord Centre (Departments of Geosciences and Physics). Fluids flow through porous media, like soils, river beds, and rocks, carrying molecules, bacteria, and particles that impact various environmental and energy processes, from carbon dioxide sequestration to groundwater remediation. While single-fluid flows (e.g., air or water) in porous media are well-understood, our ability to predict multi-fluid flows (e.g., air and water together) remains limited. This project aims to enhance our understanding of fluid dynamics in porous media through laboratory experiments. In this project, we used stereolithography 3D printing to create transparent model porous media, and imaging of fluorescent particles to measure fluid velocities. This website showcases the work that was accomplished and attests to the efforts made during the project.

Objectives

  • Design and set up experimental configurations
  • Acquire and process image data for velocity analysis
  • Analyze the flow patterns and particle behavior in multiphase systems
  • Draw conclusions and implications from the experimental results
